Diabetes Mellitus Screening

Diabetes Mellitus is a group of metabolic diseases characterized by high blood sugar levels over a prolonged period. Early detection allows for immediate lifestyle adaptations and glycemic management.

Biomarkers Analyzed:
Glucose: Plasma glucose concentration after an oral glucose tolerance test.
Blood Pressure: Diastolic blood pressure (mmHg).
BMI: Body mass index (weight in kg / (height in m)²).
Insulin: 2-Hour serum insulin (mu U/ml).
Age: Age in years.

Cardiovascular Risk Screening

Coronary heart disease remains a leading cause of mortality globally. By assessing critical hemodynamics and lipids, our models can classify risk index thresholds for cardiac episodes.

Biomarkers Analyzed:
Age & Gender: Fundamental physiological hazard parameters.
Cholesterol: Serum cholesterol density (mg/dL).
Resting Blood Pressure: Resting systolic blood pressure on admission (mmHg).
Maximum Heart Rate: Achieved cardiac rate during exercise tolerance testing.
Start Cardiology Scan

Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) Screening

Chronic Kidney Disease represents progressive loss in renal function. Because CKD is highly asymptomatic in its early stages, monitoring filtration indicators like creatinine is essential.

Biomarkers Analyzed:
Blood Urea: Waste product created by protein metabolism (mg/dL).
Serum Creatinine: Direct indicator of glomerular filtration rate (mg/dL).
Hemoglobin: Red blood cell oxygen transport protein (g/dL).
Blood Pressure: Systemic arterial blood pressure (mmHg).
Start Nephrology Scan

Hepatology (Liver) Risk Screening

Hepatic impairments such as cirrhosis, hepatitis, or fatty liver disease can lead to major systemic complications. Standard screening tools analyze bilirubin levels and specific liver enzymes to monitor structural and functional stability.

Biomarkers Analyzed:
Bilirubin: Heme breakdown waste product; signals excretion health (mg/dL).
Albumin: Primary protein synthesized by the liver (g/dL).
Alkaline Phosphatase: Enzyme related to bile ducts and bone health (IU/L).
SGOT/AST: Enzyme released upon cellular damage in liver parenchyma (IU/L).
Start Hepatology Scan
